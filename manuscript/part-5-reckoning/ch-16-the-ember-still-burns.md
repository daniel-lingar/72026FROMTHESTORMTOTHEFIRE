Chapter 16: The Ember Still Burns
For a long time, I thought I’d lost the one thing that used to make me me. My heart. Not the muscle. The part that made me want to give, to protect, to love even when I didn’t get it back.

Somewhere between the wreckage of my marriage, the manipulation I mistook for salvation, and the year I spent convinced I was clinically broken, I forgot I ever had a heart worth keeping. They handed me a Borderline Personality Disorder label. I wore it like a brand. Apologized for every feeling.

Every memory. Every desperate reaction. I isolated for a year, grieving a self I thought was fractured beyond repair. I told my kids.

I wrote pages trying to rationalize it. I told the world: This is why I am the way I am. Then, two minutes into a real therapy session, my therapist looked at me and said: “You don’t have BPD. You’re not crazy.

She manipulated you.”
The floor dropped. Again. At first, relief. Then the guilt hit hard.

If I wasn’t broken, what did that make my twenty-year marriage? What did it make all the times I pointed the finger at Trinity, blaming her control, her coldness, her emotional games for the wreckage? If I wasn’t fundamentally damaged, maybe I was the architect. Maybe I burned it down.

But truth doesn’t live in the extremes. It lives in the messy middle. Trinity did turn my kids against me. She did use them as leverage.

She did make me feel invisible in my own home, hiding me in closets like I was something to be ashamed of. Kristina did gaslight me. She did pull me in, then push me away, over and over, until I truly thought I’d lost my grip on reality. And yet...

somewhere deep down, I still felt something for them. Not trust. Not longing. Something softer than hate.

Saying “I hate them” was never the truth. It was armor. It was survival. It was my last defense against a world that kept proving connection was dangerous.

Tonight, I remembered something important—maybe the most important thing. I still have a heart. And it still wants to love. I’m tired of pretending I don’t care when I do.

I buried it under rage, grief, and self-protection. Tried to kill it with silence, with “fuck them” and “fuck me.” But it never died. It just waited. For this version of me.

The one rewriting his own story because the old script no longer fits. The black-and-white thinking told me they’re either good or evil, and I’m either the villain or the victim. That’s not real life. That’s trauma talking.

Real life is full of people who are flawed, faltering, and scared. People who hurt each other not because they’re monsters, but because they’re broken, or never taught how to love without controlling. Albert Ellis put it plainly: “People are fallible. Accept it.”
That includes Trinity.

That includes Kristina. That includes me. Maybe I’ll never get my kids back on the terms I used to imagine. Maybe the damage runs too deep for full repair right now.

But remembering I still have a heart means I can start living like I do. Not for them. Not for her. For me.

Because the fire didn’t destroy everything. The ember still burns.
