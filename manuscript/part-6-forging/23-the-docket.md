**Chapter 23 – THE DOCKET**

Two vehicles. Two jurisdictions. One nervous system that had been running on trauma wiring since the incubator.

The county case opened on paper February 11, 2022 — driving on a suspended license, no tags, no insurance. The city case reached back to June 6, 2021. Same core violation, different stops, different bureaucracies that didn’t speak to each other. They just kept generating warrants, deadlines, and consequences. The child support case (36DR-21-254) ran on its own track — opened late 2021, OCSE intervention, default judgment entered May 28/29, 2025 with income imputed to a Loves Travel Stop job I had been fired from over a year earlier. Withholding order sent June 3 to a ghost employer. Four days later, June 7, 2025, the stacked county warrants were served at JCDC and the hand injury happened. Missed trials in October and November 2025 left active warrants (CK-25-743 County, CK-25-803 City). Arrears climbed toward seven grand. Show-cause hearing set for August 10, 2026 with incarceration on the table.

On paper it looks like a man who just wouldn’t show up. From the inside it was the same freeze I learned at six under a blanket while my mom got beaten. One of those missed dates: I knew the court date, ride lined up, time closing in. Friend showed up after walking there. Asked what I was doing. Words wouldn’t come. Body locked — couldn’t move toward the shower, couldn’t speak. Getting mad at myself, furious, but the anger changed nothing. Fear of stepping outside on top of it. Agoraphobia and CPTSD tag-teaming until the window slammed shut and another warrant printed.

City and county criminal dockets plus the child support case — three separate systems, all punishing the same freeze, the same agoraphobia, the same CPTSD wiring.

I didn’t understand it at the time. That confusion — why can’t I move? Why am I scared to go outside? — pushed me into therapy. First session, diagnosis: Complex PTSD and agoraphobia. It clicked like the key in the rusted lock. Not one event. A lifetime of them. NICU wires and last rites. Violence at home. Losses stacking to twenty-seven dead people I loved. Twenty years of static in a marriage that weaponized every soft spot. The nervous system wired for danger before it learned safety.

When a court date landed, it wasn’t I don’t feel like going. It was the body locking up exactly like it did as a kid. Dorsal vagal shutdown. Prefrontal offline. The gap between I know I need to go and my body will not move. The docket doesn’t record any of that. It records non-appearance, issues the warrant, sometimes lets it sit for years, then drops multiple at once. Three systems multiplying the consequences of the same trauma responses.

The June 7, 2025 arrest wasn’t random. It was the collision point — warrants served together, hand fucked up in the process, right after the support order based on phantom income. Zero payments after that because the same freeze that missed court dates blocked consistent engagement with the paperwork and obligations. Arrears grew. August 10 is coming.

I own the damage. I caused pain. I failed appearances. I let arrears mount. But understanding the wiring stops the shame from eating me alive the way it used to. It shifts the question from Why am I so fucked up? to How did I survive this long while the systems kept compounding the cost?

This is what the docket really is: the external record of a trauma-shaped nervous system meeting fragmented bureaucracies that don’t ask why compliance failed. The same confusion that hit on those missed dates led to the therapy, the diagnosis, the digging into childhood roots, and the writing you’re holding now. This book. The other one. The WRH program. The docket forced the confrontation. The writing turned it into testimony.

The full sterile timeline and case numbers live in the Appendix. This chapter is the nervous-system map of what that timeline actually felt like from the inside.

The nervous system learned early: danger erupts without warning, freezing is survival, connection is risky. Court dates, official mail, show-cause orders — all threat cues. The body does what it was trained to do. The systems do what they’re built to do.

August 10 is coming. I’ll carry the documentation — firing records, injury medicals, therapy notes, diagnosis timeline. But even if that hearing lands right, the wiring doesn’t vanish. The work continues. The writing continues. The ember continues.

This chapter isn’t the end. It’s another page in the map I started writing the day I finally understood why I couldn’t move that morning even though I knew I had to.

The docket is still open in places. So is the nervous system. So is the story.

Turn the page anyway.
